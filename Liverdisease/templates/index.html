Introduction to Hepatic Disease

What is Hepatic Diseases..?

Liver disease is a condition that affects the liver, the body's largest organ. The liver helps digest food, store energy, and remove poisons. There are many types of liver disease, including:
Acute: Develops suddenly.
Chronic: Develops slowly over time.
Viruses: Such as hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and hepatitis C.
Inherited: Such as hemochromatosis and Wilson disease Drugs, poisons, or too much.
Alcohol: Such as fatty liver disease and cirrhosis.

Precautions For Hepatic Disease

  1. Healthy Diet:Eat fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins, limit processed meats, salt, and smoked foods to reduce Liver Disease risk.
  2. Tobacco and Alcohol: Avoid tobacco; moderate alcohol intake to lower cancer risk.
  3. Quit smoking: Smoking can increase the risk of both liver cancer and cirrhosis of the liver.
  4. Regular Screening: Schedule routine check-ups for early detection and treatment.
